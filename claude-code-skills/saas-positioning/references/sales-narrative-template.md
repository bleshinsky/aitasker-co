# Sales Narrative Template (8-Step)

Based on April Dunford's Sales Pitch methodology. This template translates
positioning into a narrative that sales reps can deliver in prospect meetings.

The narrative follows a story arc: Context → Problem → Failed Attempts →
Vision → Solution → Proof → Action.

---

## The 8 Steps

### Step 1: Setup — "The World Has Changed"

**Purpose:** Set the context. Establish a shared understanding of a market shift
or trend that the prospect is experiencing. This is NOT about your product.

**Template:**
> "You've probably noticed that [trend/shift]. For [target customer type], this
> means [specific implication]. The companies that figure this out will [positive
> outcome]. The ones that don't will [negative consequence]."

**Talk track guidance:**
- Start with something the prospect already knows and agrees with
- Use industry-specific language, not generic business speak
- The trend should connect directly to your positioning (Component 6)
- Duration: 30-60 seconds

**Key phrases to use:**
- "What we're seeing across [industry/segment] is..."
- "The shift from [old way] to [new way] means..."
- "The companies that are winning right now have figured out..."

**Phrases to avoid:**
- "Let me tell you about our company..." (too early)
- "AI is transforming everything..." (too generic)
- "We're disrupting..." (nobody cares about your disruption narrative)

**Common objection at this step:** "We're not really affected by that."
**Handle with:** "That's interesting — what's driving your [relevant metric]
right now? What's the biggest challenge your team faces with [related area]?"
(Pivot to discovery if the setup doesn't resonate.)

---

### Step 2: Problem — "What This Creates"

**Purpose:** Articulate the specific problem that the trend (Step 1) creates
for the target customer. Use their language from customer interviews.

**Template:**
> "What this creates for teams like yours is [specific problem]. We hear from
> [target persona] that [verbatim quote or paraphrase from customer interviews].
> The result is [measurable negative impact]."

**Talk track guidance:**
- Describe the problem in the prospect's language, not yours
- Be specific about the impact (time wasted, revenue lost, risk incurred)
- Reference the buyer persona's daily experience
- Duration: 45-60 seconds

**Key phrases to use:**
- "What we hear from [their role] is..."
- "The challenge this creates is..."
- "And the cost of this is [specific metric]..."

**Phrases to avoid:**
- "The problem with your current approach is..." (presumptuous)
- "You're probably doing it wrong..." (insulting)

**Transition to Step 3:** "Most teams try to solve this in one of a few ways..."

---

### Step 3: Current Approaches — "How People Try to Solve This Today"

**Purpose:** List the competitive alternatives and what each does. Show that
you understand the prospect's world and the options they have considered.

**Template:**
> "Most teams we talk to have tried some combination of:
> - [Alternative 1] — which works for [strength] but struggles with [weakness]
> - [Alternative 2] — which handles [strength] but falls short on [weakness]
> - [Alternative 3] — which is [description] but [limitation]"

**Talk track guidance:**
- List 3-4 alternatives from Component 1
- Be fair and accurate about what each does well (builds credibility)
- Then be specific about where each falls short
- Do NOT trash competitors — acknowledge their strengths
- Duration: 60-90 seconds

**Key phrases to use:**
- "Each of these approaches has merit..."
- "[Competitor] is great at [strength] — and for teams that primarily need [X], it can work"
- "The challenge comes when you need to [thing the alternative can't do]..."

**Phrases to avoid:**
- "[Competitor] is terrible..." (undermines your credibility)
- "We're better than all of them..." (too early, sounds arrogant)

**Common objection at this step:** "We're actually using [Alternative X] and it's working fine."
**Handle with:** "That makes sense — [Alternative X] is solid for [their strength]. Where we
typically see teams outgrow it is when [specific scenario that connects to your unique value].
Is that something you're experiencing, or is it more [alternative scenario]?"

---

### Step 4: Gaps — "Where They Fall Short"

**Purpose:** Identify the specific gap that none of the current approaches fill.
This gap should map directly to your unique attributes.

**Template:**
> "The gap that none of these approaches fill is [specific gap]. When [target
> customer] needs to [job-to-be-done], the current options either [limitation 1]
> or [limitation 2]. And the cost of that gap is [measurable impact]."

**Talk track guidance:**
- The gap must be real and recognized by the prospect
- Connect the gap to the unique attributes from Component 2
- Quantify the cost of the gap if possible
- Duration: 30-45 seconds

**Transition to Step 5:** "In an ideal world, what would this look like?"

---

### Step 5: The Perfect World — "Imagine If..."

**Purpose:** Paint a picture of what the ideal solution would look like. Do
this BEFORE introducing your product. This creates a buying criteria in the
prospect's mind that your product uniquely fits.

**Template:**
> "Imagine if you could [ideal outcome 1] — without [current pain point]. And
> instead of [manual process], the system automatically [desired capability].
> Your team would go from [current state] to [desired state], and you'd have
> [desired visibility/metric]."

**Talk track guidance:**
- Describe the outcome, not the features
- Use "you" language — make it about the prospect's world
- The perfect world should map exactly to your value themes (Component 3)
- Do not name your product yet
- Duration: 30-45 seconds

**Key phrases to use:**
- "Imagine if..."
- "What if your team could..."
- "Instead of [pain], you'd have [outcome]..."

---

### Step 6: Introduction — "That's Why We Built [Product]"

**Purpose:** Introduce your product, category, and primary value. One sentence.

**Template:**
> "That's why we built [Product]. [Product] is [market category] that [primary
> value proposition from positioning statement]."

**Talk track guidance:**
- ONE sentence. Do not launch into a feature tour.
- Use the category from Component 5
- Use the primary value from the positioning statement (Section 7)
- Duration: 10-15 seconds. Seriously. One sentence.

**Example:**
> "That's why we built ReliableOps. ReliableOps is a production reliability
> platform that predicts and prevents incidents before they reach your customers."

**Transition to Step 7:** "Let me show you how this works in practice..."

---

### Step 7: Value Proof — "Here's How It Works (With Evidence)"

**Purpose:** Walk through each value theme with evidence. This is where demos,
metrics, and case studies live.

**Structure:** For each value theme (2-4), deliver a mini-narrative:

> **Theme: [Value Theme Name]**
>
> "The first thing [Product] does differently is [capability connected to
> unique attribute]. For example, [Customer Name] was [situation before].
> After implementing [Product], they [specific result with metric].
>
> Let me show you what this looks like... [demo moment or screenshot]"

**Talk track guidance:**
- Lead with the value theme, not the feature
- Include a customer proof point for each theme
- Show, don't just tell (demo, screenshot, or metric)
- Map to the prospect's stakeholder altitude:
  - Executive in the room? Lead with strategic value and ROI
  - Manager? Lead with team productivity and workflow improvement
  - User? Lead with ease of use and daily experience
  - Technical evaluator? Lead with architecture, security, integration
- Duration: 3-5 minutes per theme, 10-15 minutes total

**Common objection at this step:** "How is this different from [Competitor X]?"
**Handle with:** Refer to the Unique Attributes Matrix (Section 4). Pull up the
specific attributes that differentiate you: "Great question. The key differences
are [1], [2], and [3]. Specifically, [most relevant differentiator to this
prospect's situation]."

---

### Step 8: Next Step — "Here's What Happens Next"

**Purpose:** Define a specific, low-risk next action.

**Template:**
> "The typical next step for teams like yours is [specific action]. This takes
> [time commitment] and by the end you'll [what they'll learn/get]. What does
> your calendar look like for [specific timeframe]?"

**Talk track guidance:**
- Be specific about the next step (not "let us know if you're interested")
- Match the next step to the buyer's stage:
  - Early exploration: "15-minute demo with your [relevant team member]"
  - Evaluating: "2-week free trial with your actual data"
  - Ready to decide: "1-week pilot with success criteria we define together"
- Make it low-risk and time-bounded
- Always end with a specific scheduling question
- Duration: 30-45 seconds

**Phrases to avoid:**
- "Let me send you some materials..." (too passive)
- "Think it over and get back to me..." (no commitment)
- "Would you like to see a demo?" (yes/no question invites "no")

**Better:**
- "I'd love to show your [team/person] how this works with your data. I have
  time [Tuesday or Thursday] — which works better?"

## Adapting the Narrative

### For Inbound Leads (They Came to You)
Skip Steps 1-2 (they already know the problem). Start at Step 3 with: "Before
we dive in, I'm curious — what are you using today for [job-to-be-done]?" Then
proceed from Step 3.

### For Cold Outreach (You Reached Out)
Steps 1-2 are critical — you need to earn the right to their attention. Lead
with the trend (Step 1) and problem (Step 2) that are most relevant to their
specific situation. Personalize using LinkedIn, company news, or job postings.

### For Product-Led Growth (No Sales Rep)
Translate Steps 1-7 into website and onboarding copy:
- Step 1-2 → Homepage hero and problem statement
- Step 3-4 → "Why [Product]?" or "How we're different" section
- Step 5 → Social proof section with customer outcomes
- Step 6-7 → Product tour or interactive demo
- Step 8 → CTA (free trial, sign up, get started)

### For Expansion/Upsell (Existing Customer)
Skip Steps 1-5. Start at Step 6 with the new capability, then Step 7 with
value proof relevant to their usage. Step 8 is the upgrade path.

---

## Narrative Quality Checklist

Before deploying the narrative, verify:

- [ ] Step 1 trend is something the prospect recognizes and agrees with
- [ ] Step 2 problem uses customer language, not internal jargon
- [ ] Step 3 lists alternatives fairly (does not trash competitors)
- [ ] Step 4 gap maps directly to unique attributes
- [ ] Step 5 perfect world maps to value themes
- [ ] Step 6 is one sentence (not a paragraph)
- [ ] Step 7 includes at least one proof point per value theme
- [ ] Step 8 has a specific, time-bound, low-risk next action
- [ ] Total duration: 15-25 minutes (leaves time for questions)
- [ ] A new sales rep could deliver this after one practice session
