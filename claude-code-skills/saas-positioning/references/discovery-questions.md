# Discovery Questions for SaaS Positioning

Use these questions to gather the inputs needed for the 6-component positioning
framework. Organized by audience: questions for the user/founder, questions for
customer interviews, and questions for internal team alignment.

---

## Part 1: Questions for the User / Founder

Ask these when the user invokes the positioning skill. Adapt based on their
stage (pre-launch, early traction, growth, or repositioning).

### Core Product Questions

1. **What does your product do?** (1-2 sentences, plain language)
2. **What problem does it solve?** (Not features — the actual pain it eliminates)
3. **Who is your product built for?** (Specific role, company type, situation)
4. **How does someone use your product in a typical day/week?**
5. **What is the primary outcome a customer gets from using your product?**
6. **What does your product do that competing products cannot?**
7. **When was the last time you changed your positioning or messaging? What prompted it?**

### Customer & Market Questions

8. **Who are your best customers?** (The ones who love you, expand, and refer others)
9. **What do they have in common?** (Size, industry, tech stack, situation)
10. **What were they using before they found you?**
11. **What was the trigger that made them look for a solution?** (What changed in their world?)
12. **How do customers describe your product to others?** (Their words, not yours)
13. **What is the #1 reason customers choose you over alternatives?**
14. **What is the #1 reason prospects choose a competitor instead?**
15. **Who churns, and why?** (What do churned customers have in common?)

### Competitive Questions

16. **Who do you consider your top 3 competitors?**
17. **How do you position against each?** (What do you say when a prospect asks "how are you different from X?")
18. **Is there a competitor you lose to most often? Why?**
19. **What category do you put yourself in?** (What do you write on industry forms?)
20. **Do prospects understand that category immediately, or do you have to explain it?**

### Business Model Questions

21. **What is your pricing model?** (Per-seat, usage-based, flat-rate, freemium)
22. **What is your average deal size / ACV?**
23. **How long is your typical sales cycle?**
24. **Is your go-to-market product-led (self-serve) or sales-led (human-driven)?**
25. **What stage are you at?** (Pre-launch / Early traction <$1M ARR / Growth $1M-$10M / Scale $10M+)

### Aspiration Questions

26. **Where do you want to be in 12 months?** (Market position, customer profile, category)
27. **If you could be known for one thing, what would it be?**
28. **What is the single biggest obstacle to growth right now?**

## Part 2: Customer Interview Questions

Use these when conducting customer research as input to positioning.
Interview 5-8 customers who purchased in the last 90 days.

### Context & Trigger

1. **Tell me about your role. What are you responsible for?**
   *(Establishes persona, seniority, and metrics they care about)*

2. **Before you started using [Product], how were you handling [job-to-be-done]?**
   *(Reveals competitive alternatives — their answer, not yours)*

3. **Walk me through a typical day/week with the old approach. What did that look like?**
   *(Reveals pain points and workflow context)*

4. **What was the moment you decided the old way wasn't working anymore? Was there a specific event or realization?**
   *(Reveals the trigger — the push force in JTBD)*

### Search & Evaluation

5. **How did you first hear about [Product]? What caught your attention?**
   *(Reveals acquisition channel and initial messaging that resonated)*

6. **What other options did you consider? How far did you get with each?**
   *(Reveals competitive set from the buyer's perspective)*

7. **What almost stopped you from switching? What concerns did you have?**
   *(Reveals anxiety forces — objections and barriers to address in positioning)*

8. **What was the deciding factor? What tipped you toward [Product]?**
   *(Reveals the primary differentiator from the customer's perspective)*

### Value & Outcome

9. **What can you do now that you couldn't do before?**
   *(Reveals value themes in customer language)*

10. **If you had to describe [Product] to a colleague in one sentence, what would you say?**
    *(Reveals how customers naturally position you — this is gold for messaging)*

11. **What's the biggest impact [Product] has had on your work/team?**
    *(Reveals the primary value theme and potential proof points)*

12. **If [Product] disappeared tomorrow, what would you switch to?**
    *(The single most important competitive intelligence question — reveals true alternatives)*

### Meta Questions

13. **Is there anything about [Product] that surprised you — something you didn't expect?**
    *(May reveal unique value you are not aware of)*

14. **What would make you leave [Product]?**
    *(Reveals risk factors and competitive vulnerabilities)*

15. **Is there anyone else I should talk to on your team who uses [Product] differently?**
    *(May reveal additional personas or use cases)*

### Interview Tips

- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes per interview
- **Recording:** Always ask permission to record. Transcripts are invaluable.
- **Silence:** After they answer, wait 3-5 seconds. They often add the most
  valuable insight in the pause.
- **Follow up:** "Tell me more about that" and "Can you give me a specific
  example?" are the two most powerful follow-up questions.
- **Avoid leading:** Do not ask "Don't you think [Product] is better because...?"
  Let them tell you what matters.

## Part 3: Internal Team Alignment Questions

Ask these to key team members (CEO, head of sales, head of product, head of
marketing) to surface internal misalignment before building positioning.

### Alignment Check

1. **How do you describe what we do in one sentence?**
   *(Ask each person separately. Compare answers. Misalignment here is the #1
   sign that positioning work is needed.)*

2. **Who is our ideal customer?**
   *(If the sales team, product team, and marketing team give different answers,
   you have a targeting problem.)*

3. **What are we best at?**
   *(Reveals perceived strengths — compare to what customers actually say.)*

4. **What is our biggest competitive advantage?**
   *(If the team cannot agree, the positioning will be muddled.)*

5. **What category are we in?**
   *(If internal answers differ, external perception is definitely confused.)*

6. **What is the one thing we want prospects to remember about us?**
   *(Tests message clarity and alignment.)*

### Sales Team Specific

7. **What objection do you hear most often?**
   *(Reveals where positioning fails to preempt concerns.)*

8. **What competitor do you lose to most often, and why?**
   *(May differ from leadership's perception.)*

9. **What type of customer closes fastest and expands most?**
   *(Data-driven ICP refinement.)*

10. **When you win a competitive deal, what was the deciding factor?**
    *(Reveals the differentiator that actually matters in deals, which may
    differ from what marketing emphasizes.)*

### Product Team Specific

11. **What features do users engage with most? Least?**
    *(Usage data reveals what is actually valuable vs. what is assumed.)*

12. **What do customers request most on the roadmap?**
    *(Reveals unmet needs — may inform future positioning.)*

13. **What is the hardest thing to explain about our product?**
    *(Signals category or messaging confusion.)*

## Part 4: Clustering and Pattern Matching

After gathering responses, cluster the data to feed the 6 components:

| Input Source | Maps To Component |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Customer Q2, Q12 ("What were you using before?", "What would you switch to?") | **Component 1: Competitive Alternatives** |
| Customer Q8 ("Deciding factor"), Internal Q3-Q4 ("Best at", "Advantage") | **Component 2: Unique Attributes** |
| Customer Q9, Q10, Q11 ("What can you do now?", "One sentence", "Biggest impact") | **Component 3: Value Mapping** |
| Customer Q1, Q4 ("Role", "Trigger"), Founder Q8-Q11, Sales Q9 ("Fastest close") | **Component 4: Best-Fit Customers** |
| Founder Q19-Q20 ("Category"), Internal Q5, Customer Q10 ("One sentence") | **Component 5: Market Category** |
| Founder Q4 ("Trigger"), Customer Q4 ("Old way not working") | **Component 6: Relevant Trends** |

### Red Flags in Discovery Data

- **Customers describe you differently than you describe yourself** — Your
  positioning is out of sync with reality. Use their description, not yours.
- **Sales team and product team give different ICPs** — Internal misalignment
  will produce confused external positioning. Resolve before building.
- **"We have no competitors"** — You always have competitors. If customers
  would do nothing without you, then "do nothing" is your competitor.
- **Every customer interview reveals a different "deciding factor"** — Your
  differentiation is unclear, or your customer base is too diverse. Narrow
  the target to find a pattern.
- **The team cannot agree on the category** — Category confusion internally
  guarantees category confusion externally. This must be resolved as part
  of the positioning work.
