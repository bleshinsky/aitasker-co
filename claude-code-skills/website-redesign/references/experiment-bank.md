## Homepage Experiments

### Hero Section
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Headline variations | Specific outcome-focused vs. abstract messaging — specificity typically wins |
| Subheadline clarity | Adding/refining supporting copy improves comprehension and conversion |
| CTA above fold | Including a prominent CTA in the hero reduces time-to-conversion |
| Hero visual format | Screenshot vs. GIF vs. illustration vs. video — product screenshots often outperform illustrations |
| CTA button color | Higher contrast against the background increases click-through |
| CTA button text | "Start Free Trial" vs. "Get Started" vs. "See Demo" — more specific copy tends to convert better |
| Interactive demo | Engaging visitors immediately with the product reduces bounce and increases trial starts |

### Trust & Social Proof
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Logo placement | Logos in the hero section build instant credibility vs. below-fold placement |
| Case study in hero | Showing specific results immediately increases confidence |
| Trust badges | Security, compliance, and award badges increase form completion |
| Social proof in headline | "Join 10,000+ teams" messaging adds urgency and validation |
| Testimonial placement | Above-fold testimonials convert better than dedicated sections lower on the page |
| Video testimonials | More engaging and credible than text quotes |

### Features & Content
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Feature presentation | Icons + brief descriptions vs. detailed expandable sections — depends on audience sophistication |
| Section ordering | Moving highest-value features/benefits up increases scroll depth and conversion |
| Secondary CTAs | Adding CTAs after each feature section captures visitors at their peak interest |
| Benefit vs. feature focus | Benefit-led copy ("Save 10 hours/week") outperforms feature-led ("Automated workflows") |
| Comparison section | Explicit comparison vs. competitors or status quo helps fence-sitters decide |

### Navigation & UX
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Sticky navigation | Persistent nav with CTA keeps the conversion path always visible |
| Nav CTA button | A prominent button in the nav bar increases overall conversion rate |
| Exit intent popup | Capturing abandoning visitors with a targeted offer recovers lost conversions |

## Pricing Page Experiments

### Price Presentation
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Annual vs. monthly display | Leading with annual pricing (with savings shown) increases annual plan selection |
| Price point psychology | $99 vs. $100 vs. $97 — just-below pricing affects perceived value |
| "Most Popular" badge | Highlighting the target plan increases its selection rate |
| Number of tiers | 3 tiers is the sweet spot; 4+ creates decision paralysis |
| Price anchoring | Showing the expensive plan first makes the middle plan feel like a deal |

### Pricing UX
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Pricing calculator | Usage-based pricing clarity reduces "contact sales" drop-off |
| Feature comparison format | Tables work for technical buyers; expandable sections for less technical |
| Monthly/annual toggle | With savings percentage highlighted increases annual selection |
| Plan recommendation quiz | Interactive "which plan is right for you?" reduces decision anxiety |

### Objection Handling
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| FAQ section | Addressing common pricing objections directly on the page reduces bounce |
| ROI calculator | Showing concrete value vs. cost justifies the price in the visitor's mind |
| Money-back guarantee | Prominent placement near the CTA reduces perceived risk |
| Competitor comparison | Side-by-side value comparison helps visitors who are evaluating alternatives |

### Trust on Pricing
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Value-focused testimonials | Quotes specifically about ROI placed near pricing tiers increase selection |
| Customer logos near pricing | Familiar brand logos next to pricing reduce price sensitivity |
| Review scores | G2/Capterra ratings add third-party credibility |
| Case study snippet | Specific pricing/value results near the CTA validate the investment |

## Landing Page Experiments

### Message Match
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Headline matching | Headline that exactly mirrors the ad copy improves quality score and conversion |
| Visual matching | Landing page creative matching the ad creative reduces dissonance |
| Audience-specific pages | Segment-specific pages outperform one-size-fits-all by speaking to specific pain points |

### Conversion Focus
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Navigation removal | Removing all navigation forces focus on the single conversion goal |
| CTA repetition | Multiple CTAs throughout the page catch visitors at different decision points |
| Form vs. button | Direct email capture vs. click-through to signup — depends on traffic temperature |
| Urgency/scarcity | Genuine limited-time offers or capacity limits create action motivation |
| Short vs. long page | Warm traffic converts on short pages; cold traffic needs the full argument |

### Page Structure
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Above-fold only | Minimal-scroll pages with everything above the fold work for high-intent traffic |
| Section ordering | Leading with strongest proof element increases time-on-page |
| Footer removal | Eliminating footer navigation keeps attention on conversion |

## Demo Request Page Experiments

### Form Optimization
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Field count | Reducing from 6+ fields to 3-4 increases form completion rate significantly |
| Multi-step vs. single | Multi-step with progress bar feels easier than one long form |
| Form placement | Above-fold form captures high-intent visitors immediately |
| Phone field | Removing the phone field increases completion (add it to a follow-up step) |
| Field enrichment | Auto-filling company data from email domain reduces manual entry |

### Page Content
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Benefits above form | Reinforcing value immediately before the ask reduces abandonment |
| Demo preview | Video/GIF showing what the demo experience is like sets expectations and excites |
| "What You'll Learn" | Clear expectations increase form completion |
| Testimonials near form | Social proof at the decision point reduces friction |
| FAQ below form | Addressing common objections catches visitors who scroll past the form |

### CTA & Routing
| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| CTA text | "Book Your Demo" vs. "Schedule 15-Min Call" — specificity about time reduces perceived commitment |
| On-demand option | Offering instant self-serve demo alongside live option captures both preferences |
| Calendar integration | Inline booking (Calendly-style) vs. "we'll email you" — inline converts better |
| Qualification routing | Routing smaller teams to self-serve and enterprises to sales optimizes both paths |

## Feature Page Experiments

| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Product demo/screenshot | Showing the feature in action with real UI increases comprehension |
| Use case examples | Concrete "how customers use this" stories drive more trials than abstract descriptions |
| Before/after | Visual impact comparison makes the benefit tangible |
| Interactive demo | Try-the-feature without signup reduces barrier to understanding |
| Video walkthrough | Feature tour videos increase time-on-page and trial conversion |
| Competitor comparison | Direct comparison with competitors' version positions the feature advantage |
| Trial CTA | Feature-specific trial offer ("Try [feature] free") outperforms generic "Start trial" |
| Related features | Cross-linking to complementary features increases pages-per-session |

## Blog/Resource Page Experiments

| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Floating sticky CTA | Persistent sidebar or bottom CTA maintains conversion path during reading |
| Inline CTA placement | CTAs at natural stopping points (after key insights) outperform end-of-post only |
| Content upgrades | Topic-specific downloadables ("Get the full checklist") outperform generic newsletter CTAs |
| Gated vs. free | Strategic gating of premium content generates leads; over-gating hurts SEO |
| Reading time display | Showing estimated reading time increases article completion |
| Related resources | End-of-article recommendations increase session depth |
| Topic bundles | Grouped resources by theme increase engagement and perceived authority |

## Cross-Page / Site-Wide Experiments

| Test | Hypothesis |
|------|------------|
| Chat widget | Live chat or AI chatbot increases conversion by answering questions in real-time |
| Cookie consent UX | Minimizing cookie banner friction prevents early-page abandonment |
| Page load speed | Faster load directly correlates with higher conversion — every second counts |
| Mobile experience | Mobile-specific optimizations (sticky bottom CTA, simplified nav) lift mobile conversion |
| Accessibility | Improving accessibility expands the audience that can convert |
| Personalization | Dynamic content by visitor segment (industry, company size, traffic source) increases relevance |
| CTA in navigation | Always-visible CTA button in the nav bar increases overall site conversion |
| Search placement | Helping visitors find relevant content faster reduces bounce |
| Breadcrumbs | Navigation clarity reduces frustration on deep-content sites |
